In [30]:
plt.figure(figsize=(8,6))
ax = sns.countplot(x='Churn', data=df)

for container in ax.containers:
    ax.bar_label(container)

plt.title("Customer Churn Distribution")
plt.show()

Observation¶

  • Out of 7,032 customers, 5,163 customers stayed with the company while 1,869 customers churned. This indicates that the majority of customers were retained, but approximately 27% of customers left the company, making churn a significant business concern.

Business Insight¶

  • Since nearly one out of every four customers churned, the company should identify the factors influencing customer attrition and develop retention strategies to improve customer loyalty.

Business Insight¶

  • Gender does not appear to have a significant impact on customer churn, as both male and female customers show nearly identical churn patterns. Other factors such as contract type, tenure, monthly charges, or payment method are likely to have a greater influence on customer churn.

3. Contract Type vs Customer Churn¶

In [35]:
plt.figure(figsize=(10,6))

ax = sns.countplot(x='Contract', data=df, hue='Churn')

for container in ax.containers:
    ax.bar_label(container)

plt.xlabel("Contract Type")
plt.ylabel("Number of Customers")
plt.show()

Observation¶

  • Customers with month-to-month contracts have the highest number of churn cases (1,655). In contrast, customers with one-year and two-year contracts have significantly lower churn counts (166 and 48 respectively).

Business Insight¶

  • Contract type is a major factor influencing customer churn. Customers on month-to-month contracts are much more likely to leave the company than those on longer-term contracts. Encouraging customers to switch to one-year or two-year contracts could help improve customer retention and reduce churn.

plt.ylabel("Tenure (Months)")
plt.show()

Observation¶

  • Customers who churned generally have a much lower tenure than customers who stayed. The median tenure for churned customers is around 9–10 months, whereas customers who remained with the company have a median tenure of approximately 38 months.

Business Insight¶

  • Tenure is a strong indicator of customer churn. Customers are more likely to leave during the early months of their subscription. Improving the onboarding experience and engaging customers during their first year may help reduce churn.

10. Correlation Heatmap¶

In [66]:
plt.figure(figsize=(8,6))

sns.heatmap(
    df[['SeniorCitizen', 'tenure', 'MonthlyCharges', 'TotalCharges']].corr(),
    annot=True,
    cmap='coolwarm',
    fmt='.2f'
)

plt.title("Correlation Heatmap")
plt.show()

Observation¶

  • The heatmap shows the correlation between the numerical features in the dataset. Tenure and TotalCharges have a strong positive correlation (0.83), indicating that customers who stay longer tend to accumulate higher total charges. MonthlyCharges and TotalCharges also show a moderate positive correlation (0.65). SeniorCitizen has only weak correlations with the other numerical variables.

Business Insight¶

  • The strongest relationship is between Tenure and TotalCharges, which is expected because customers who remain with the company for a longer time naturally accumulate higher total charges. No strong relationship exists between SeniorCitizen and the other numerical features.
